About Staks Rosch

Staks Rosch is a Philadelphia-based atheist activist, writer, and philosopher known for promoting secular humanism and reason. Originally from New Jersey, Rosch attended West Chester University, where he earned a Master's Degree in Philosophy. Over the last decade, he has emerged as a leading voice in the atheist and humanist community, blending intellectual rigor with grassroots activism.

Born in northern New Jersey, Rosch has been active in atheist and progressive circles since his college years. Today, he identifies as a Secular Humanist, skeptic, and rationalist, emphasizing that he "believes in people" rather than gods. This human-centered philosophy underpins all his activism and writing.

As the former head of the Philadelphia Coalition of Reason and creator of the influential DangerousTalk blog, Staks has dedicated his career to advocating for humanist values and critical thinking in public life. He has fought for the rights of minorities, women, and the LGBTQ+ community as part of his broader commitment to social justice.

"I believe in people, not gods. There is no society in human history that ever suffered because its people became too reasonable."

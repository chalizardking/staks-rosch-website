Biography

About Staks Rosch

Atheist Activist, Writer, and Philosopher

Who is Staks Rosch?

Staks Rosch is a Philadelphia-based atheist activist, writer, and philosopher known for promoting secular humanism and reason. Originally from New Jersey, Rosch attended West Chester University, where he earned a Master's Degree in Philosophy. During graduate school, he became deeply involved in campus and community freethought activities.

Over the last decade, Rosch has emerged as a leading voice in the atheist and humanist community, blending intellectual rigor with grassroots activism. He advocates for humanist values and critical thinking in public life, and has fought for the rights of minorities, women, and the LGBTQ+ community as part of his broader commitment to social justice.

A Personal Journey to Atheism

Born in northern New Jersey, Rosch has been active in atheist and progressive circles since his college years. Today, Rosch identifies as a Secular Humanist, skeptic, and rationalist, emphasizing that he "believes in people" rather than gods. This human-centered philosophy underpins his activism and writing.

"I believe in people, not gods. There is no society in human history that ever suffered because its people became too reasonable."

While strongly critical of religion, he rejects stereotypes of "militant atheism" and instead promotes education and dialogue to diminish religion's influence. By combining academic background in philosophy with a passion for civic engagement, Staks Rosch has made a unique mark as both a thinker and an organizer in America's secular movement.

Media Presence & Public Advocacy

In media appearances, Rosch has represented atheist viewpoints across various platforms. He was a panelist on the TV program "It's Your Call with Lynn Doyle" (discussing Intelligent Design in schools) and even appeared on Christian talk radio to debate the merits of atheism. These engagements demonstrate his willingness to step into challenging conversations and defend secular perspectives in diverse settings.

Leadership in the Secular Community

As the former head of the Philadelphia Coalition of Reason (PhillyCoR), Staks has worked to build and strengthen the secular community in the greater Philadelphia area. He has served on the board of the Freethought Society of Greater Philadelphia and has been instrumental in organizing events that bring together like-minded individuals who value reason and evidence-based thinking.

Staks is perhaps best known for his "Dangerous Talk" blog, which he founded and has maintained for years on the Skeptic Ink Network. Through this platform, he has published hundreds of articles on atheism, humanism, politics, and social issues, reaching thousands of readers worldwide. His writing often combines humor with pointed critiques of religious doctrine and advocacy of skepticism.

Author & Philosopher

In March 2021, Staks published his book, "Disproving God and 5 Adequate Reasons To Be an Atheist," a concise 58-page work intended as an accessible introduction to atheism. Drawing on his 30+ years of experience as an atheist activist, Rosch leverages his extensive knowledge of religious arguments and counter-arguments to formulate "an argument that is simple and irrefutable," delivered with his characteristic humor.

The book has been praised by secular activists like Janice Rael as "accessible, approachable atheism" and "a fresh look at a timeless question: Does God exist?" Futurist author Zoltan Istvan called it "a true benefit to humanity" that makes a "simple and convincing case against the supernatural."

Creative Activism

In 2025, Rosch launched an innovative project: Reason+1 (pronounced "Reason Plus One"), an atheist-themed AI music band. The debut album, "That Ungodly Sound," was released on Memorial Day 2025, featuring rock songs with provocative titles like "Don't Need Jesus To Save My Soul," "God is Imaginary," "Blasphemy," and "Heart Over Heaven." This experimental musical endeavor represents Rosch's willingness to explore new forms of outreach and creative expression in spreading secular messages.

Core Philosophy

With a formal education in philosophy and decades of experience in activism, Rosch approaches these issues with both academic rigor and practical wisdom. His versatility as a thinker, organizer, and communicator—spanning books, blogs, radio, public speaking, and even AI-generated music—has made Staks Rosch a familiar and respected name in secular circles. He continues to advocate for reason, compassion, and evidence-based thinking in all aspects of public life.
